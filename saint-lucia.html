The island, with its fine natural harbor at Castries and burgeoning sugar industry, was contested by England and France throughout the 17th and early 18th centuries (changing possession 14 times); it was finally ceded to the UK in 1814 and became part of the British Windward Islands colony. Even after the abolition of slavery on its plantations in 1834, Saint Lucia remained an agricultural island, dedicated to producing tropical commodity crops.

In the mid-20th century, Saint Lucia joined the West Indies Federation and in 1967 became one of the six members of the West Indies Associated States, with internal self-government. In 1979, Saint Lucia gained full independence.

Economy

Economic Overview

Saint Lucia is a tourism-based Caribbean island economy that is environmentally fragile. The nation is an energy import-dependent and a major banana producer.

It has a well-educated labor force and it's key infrastructure are Information Technology (IT) and communications investments.

Government

Government Type

Saint Lucia is a parliamentary democracy under a constitutional monarchy. The nation is also known as a Commonwealth realm, which means the King or Queen of the United Kingdom acts as monarch of the nation.

Elections

The monarchy is hereditary, which means the throne passes from one member of the ruling family to a family member.

The governor general is appointed by the Monarch following legislative elections.

The leader of the majority party or majority coalition is usually appointed prime minister by the governor general and the deputy prime minister is appointed by the governor general.
